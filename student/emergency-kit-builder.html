Introduction to Emergency Kit Building

An emergency kit is a collection of basic items your household may need in the event of an emergency. Having an emergency kit ready can help you be self-sufficient for at least 72 hours during an emergency when you may not have access to help from emergency services.

Why Emergency Kits Matter

During disasters, normal services like electricity, water, and communication may be disrupted. Emergency responders may not be able to reach you immediately. Your emergency kit ensures you can take care of yourself and your family until help arrives.

Basic Emergency Kit Supplies

Essential Water and Food Supplies

Water Requirements

  • Amount: Store at least 1 gallon of water per person per day for at least 3 days
  • Storage: Use food-grade water storage containers
  • Rotation: Replace stored water every 6 months
  • Purification: Include water purification tablets or a portable water filter

Food Supplies (3-day minimum per person)

  • Non-perishable food items (canned goods, dried fruits, nuts)
  • Manual can opener
  • High-energy foods (protein bars, crackers)
  • Special dietary needs (baby formula, medications)

Safety and First Aid Supplies

First Aid Kit Components

A well-stocked first aid kit is essential for treating minor injuries and stabilizing more serious conditions until professional medical help arrives.

Basic First Aid Supplies

  • Adhesive bandages in various sizes
  • Sterile gauze pads and medical tape
  • Antiseptic wipes and ointment
  • Pain relievers (aspirin, ibuprofen, acetaminophen)
  • Thermometer
  • Disposable gloves
  • Emergency blanket
  • Instant cold compress

Prescription Medications

  • 7-day supply of prescription medications
  • Medical alert bracelet or list of medical conditions
  • Contact information for doctors and pharmacies

Pro Tip

Check expiration dates on medications and first aid supplies every 6 months and replace as needed.

Medical Info

Keep a list of medical conditions, allergies, and emergency contacts in a waterproof container.

Essential Tools and Equipment

Communication and Lighting

Communication Devices

  • Emergency Radio: Battery-powered or hand-crank radio for emergency broadcasts
  • Cell Phone Chargers: Portable battery packs and solar chargers
  • Whistle: For signaling help
  • Two-way Radios: For family communication if cell towers are down

Lighting and Power

  • Flashlights (one per person) with extra batteries
  • Lanterns for area lighting
  • Candles and waterproof matches
  • Solar chargers or hand-crank generators

Basic Tools

  • Multi-tool or Swiss Army knife
  • Duct tape and plastic sheeting
  • Work gloves
  • Rope or paracord
  • Adjustable wrench for turning off utilities

Battery Management

Store batteries separately from devices to prevent corrosion. Test all battery-powered equipment monthly and replace batteries as needed. Consider rechargeable batteries with solar chargers for sustainable power.

Personal Care and Clothing

Hygiene and Sanitation

Personal Hygiene Items

  • Soap, toothbrushes, and toothpaste
  • Feminine hygiene products
  • Personal medications
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Toilet paper and facial tissues
  • Disposable plates, cups, and utensils

Clothing and Bedding

  • Change of clothing for each person (seasonal appropriate)
  • Sturdy shoes or boots
  • Rain gear and warm clothing
  • Sleeping bags or blankets
  • Pillows or inflatable pillows

Special Considerations

  • Infants: Diapers, formula, bottles, baby food
  • Elderly: Extra medications, assistive devices
  • Pets: Food, water, medications, carriers

Important Documents and Cash

Essential Documentation

Critical Documents (Keep copies in waterproof container)

  • Identification documents (driver's license, passport, birth certificates)
  • Insurance policies (home, auto, health, life)
  • Medical records and prescription information
  • Bank account and credit card information
  • Emergency contact list
  • Local area maps

Cash and Credit

  • Cash in small bills
  • Coins for pay phones
  • Credit cards
  • Traveler's checks

Digital Backup

Store digital copies of important documents in cloud storage or on encrypted USB drives.

Security

Keep documents in a fireproof and waterproof safe or security deposit box.

Specialized Emergency Kits

Location-Specific Kits

Vehicle Emergency Kit

  • Jumper cables and tire repair kit
  • Emergency flares or reflectors
  • Ice scraper and snow shovel
  • Tow rope and emergency escape tool
  • Maps (paper copies)
  • Extra motor oil and coolant

Workplace Emergency Kit

  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Small food supply (3 days)
  • Water and water purification tablets
  • Small first aid kit
  • Emergency contact information
  • Medications

School Emergency Kit

  • Emergency contact card
  • Medical information and medications
  • Comfort items (small toy, photo)
  • Emergency snacks
  • Change of clothes

Kit Maintenance and Storage

Proper Storage Techniques

Storage Best Practices

  • Location: Store in a cool, dry place easily accessible to all family members
  • Containers: Use airtight, waterproof containers or bags
  • Organization: Label everything clearly and keep inventory list
  • Accessibility: Ensure kits are portable and easy to carry

Regular Maintenance Schedule

  • Monthly: Test battery-powered equipment
  • Every 6 months: Replace water, check expiration dates
  • Annually: Update documents, replace expired items, reassess needs

Inventory Management

  • Keep a detailed inventory list with expiration dates
  • Rotate perishable items using first-in, first-out principle
  • Update kit contents based on seasonal changes
  • Adjust for family changes (new members, moved out, medical changes)

Maintenance Reminder

Set calendar reminders for kit maintenance. Many families choose daylight saving time changes as reminders to check their emergency kits.
